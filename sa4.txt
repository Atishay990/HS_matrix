 • In 1973, sociologist Daniel Bell noted that a new type of society was emerging: Post-Industrial
society.
 • A service sector so large that most people work in it.
 • A vast surplus of goods; even more extensive trade among nations.
 • A wider variety and quantity of goods available to the average person.
 • An information explosion.
 • An interconnected Global village—that is, the world’s nations are linked by fast communications,
transportation and trade.
